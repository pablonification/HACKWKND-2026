EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
Every two weeks, the world loses a language, and with it disappears centuries of cultural knowledge, identity, and history. According to UNESCO, nearly 40% of the world’s approximately 7,000 languages are endangered, and many could disappear within the next century if no preservation efforts are undertaken. Southeast Asia is one of the most linguistically diverse regions in the world, with over 1,200 indigenous languages, yet the rapid growth of digital technologies has created a linguistic divide where only a small number of global languages dominate online platforms. In fact, studies suggest that less than 5% of the world’s languages have sufficient digital representation, leaving most indigenous languages excluded from modern technological ecosystems.
The Semai language, spoken by the Orang Asli community in Malaysia, faces the threat of digital extinction as younger generations shift toward mainstream languages and fragile oral traditions fade. To address this, the project introduces Taleka, an AI-powered platform designed to preserve and teach the language by transforming it into a structured digital ecosystem. Taleka bridges the generational gap by enabling elders to archive cultural knowledge through the Elder Studio, while younger users engage with the language through interactive features like the Language Garden, gamified activities, and the Tavi AI Helper. Ultimately, the platform ensures this vital indigenous heritage remains accessible and vibrant in the digital age.
The project also demonstrates promising financial feasibility. Based on the financial projection analysis, Taleka is expected to generate a Total Net Profit of $85,009.35 with a Total Cost of $300,012.71, resulting in a Return on Investment (ROI) of 28.34%. With a Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) of 10%, the project achieves a positive Net Present Value (NPV) of $19,061.53 with the Payback Period (PBP) of 3 years, indicating that the investment is financially viable and capable of generating sustainable long-term returns.
Overall, Taleka presents an innovative approach to indigenous language revitalization by combining AI-driven technology, cultural storytelling, and interactive learning experiences. By doing so, the platform contributes to SDG 4 (Quality Education) through mother-tongue learning and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities) by protecting intangible cultural heritage. Taleka ultimately aims to ensure that the Semai language—and other indigenous languages across Southeast Asia—can survive and thrive in the digital era.

CHAPTER 1
INTRODUCTION
Background
Southeast Asia is a global epicenter of linguistic diversity, home to over 1,200 languages that serve as repositories for unique indigenous knowledge and oral histories. However, this heritage is under immediate threat that UNESCO indicates more than 40% of the world’s languages are currently endangered. In the ASEAN region, the rapid expansion of digital technology often ignores indigenous voices, creating a digital ecosystem that supports only high-resource national languages.

Figure 1.1 Semai as an Indigenous Cultural Landscape in Peninsular Malaysia
A primary example is the Semai language of Peninsular Malaysia. Spoken by approximately 40,000–50,000 members of the Orang Asli community, the language remains vulnerable because it is transmitted almost exclusively through oral traditions. In communities like Kampung Dayok, the shift toward dominant languages in formal education and digital media is causing Semai to lose its relevance among younger generations. Without systematic digital intervention, this unique linguistic identity faces "digital extinction."
Chosen SDG

Figure 1.2 Quality Education, Sustainable Cities and Communities
For the Semai people, cultural identity is woven into an oral tradition that remains profoundly beautiful yet increasingly fragile in a digital-first world. This project bridges the gap between ancestral wisdom and modern technology by leveraging artificial intelligence to advance two critical UN Sustainable Development Goals:
SDG 4 (Quality Education) – Ensuring inclusive education by providing mother-tongue learning resources that improve literacy and academic performance for indigenous students.
SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities and Communities) – Safeguarding the world’s cultural heritage by digitizing and preserving oral traditions for future generations.
By transforming oral histories into accessible digital tools, the initiative ensures the Semai language does not just survive the digital age but thrives also.

Problem Statement
Linguistic erosion and digital exclusion are rapidly silencing the Semai community. This happens as the absence of native language resources forces their ancestral wisdom to fade in a digital ecosystem dominated by national languages. While formal education and digital platforms increasingly favor these dominant national and global languages, indigenous students face significant learning barriers. Semai language is one of them. The language loses its presence in modern environments because the language lacks structured digital documentation and interactive technologies. Since these indigenous languages aren’t equipped with speech recognition and machine translation, the community's rich oral histories and cultural identity are at risk of being permanently lost.

Objectives
The project aims to revitalize the Semai language and empower its speakers through the following objectives:
Digital Archiving: Utilizing AI to systematically transcribe oral traditions, creating a permanent digital corpus to effectively prevent linguistic extinction.
AI-Enhanced Acquisition: Driving youth fluency through gamified learning and AI-powered conversational tools tailored for indigenous language revitalization.
Heritage Connectivity: Bridging generational divides by leveraging AI-driven storytelling to transfer ancestral cultural knowledge from rural elders to urban youth.
Inclusive Infrastructure: Ensuring remote accessibility through a robust offline-first architecture to overcome systemic connectivity barriers for isolated indigenous communities.

REVIEW OF EXISTING SOLUTIONS

Current Solutions and Market Analysis
Currently, language learning platforms such as Duolingo dominate the global digital language education market. Duolingo offers interactive lessons for dozens of languages through gamified exercises, making language learning accessible to millions of users worldwide. Interestingly, the platform has demonstrated the flexibility of its system by offering courses not only for widely spoken languages but also for less commonly used or even fictional languages.
For example, Duolingo launched a course for High Valyrian, a fictional language from the Game of Thrones universe. The course was introduced in 2019 and attracted over 500,000 active learners, demonstrating that digital language-learning platforms can successfully support niche languages if there is sufficient interest and well-designed learning content. Even earlier reports showed that nearly 100,000 people in the UK alone signed up to learn High Valyrian, highlighting the strong demand for unique language-learning experiences.

Figure 2.1 Duolingo as a language learning app
However, a major gap remains in the digital education ecosystem. Mainstream platforms rarely support indigenous or low-resource languages, particularly those spoken by smaller communities in Southeast Asia. Languages such as Semai, spoken by the Orang Asli communities in Peninsular Malaysia, remain largely absent from digital learning platforms due to limited linguistic datasets, lack of commercial incentives, and technological barriers. Existing initiatives focused on indigenous language preservation often prioritize documentation and archival efforts, such as recording oral stories or compiling dictionaries. While these efforts are valuable for cultural preservation, they frequently lack the interactive and scalable educational tools required to engage younger generations who are accustomed to mobile apps and digital learning experiences.

This gap between static preservation initiatives and modern digital learning platforms presents a significant opportunity for specialized solutions that integrate language preservation with engaging educational technology. Taleka addresses this gap by combining AI-powered language processing, community-led knowledge collection, and gamified learning tools to support both language documentation and active language revitalization.

Limitations
Despite the proposed system offering solutions for Semai language preservation and learning, several limitations remain.
Limited Vocabulary Coverage in Elder Studio
Elder’s Studio feature is still limited in handling vocabulary that is not available in the current database. This limitation exists because the available digital resources for the Semai language on the internet are still very limited, resulting in a relatively small vocabulary dataset. As a result, the system may not always recognize or process words that fall outside the existing database. To address this gap in the future, the platform can implement a community contribution mechanism, where users are allowed to submit new vocabulary entries. These entries can then be verified by moderators or linguistic experts before being added to the database, gradually expanding the language corpus.
Absence of Text-to-Speech (TTS) Support
Another limitation of the current system is the absence of a text-to-speech (TTS) feature for the Semai language. This is primarily due to the lack of publicly available speech datasets and pretrained speech synthesis models for low-resource indigenous languages like Semai. Developing an accurate TTS system requires a large amount of annotated audio data, which is currently scarce. Consequently, the platform currently focuses on text-based learning and transcription features. Future work may address this limitation by collecting community voice recordings to build a speech dataset that can support the development of Semai text-to-speech technology.

CHAPTER 3
METHODOLOGY
Solution Overview
Taleka is an AI-powered language preservation and learning platform designed to help indigenous communities move from fragile oral transmission to a structured, interactive digital ecosystem for cultural and linguistic continuity. It transforms scattered oral knowledge such as stories, riddles, and everyday speech into organized, accessible digital learning resources tailored to the phonetic and cultural nuances of the Semai language.
The platform collects linguistic signals from the community, including spoken narratives, riddles, and conversational phrases, and organizes them into structured language modules that can be explored through interactive storytelling and gamified learning. AI models support speech recognition, pronunciation guidance, and contextual translation to help users learn and practice the language while preserving authentic pronunciation and cultural meaning.

Figure 3.1 The difference in before and after implementing Taleka

The core output is a living digital language archive and learning system that contains verified Semai vocabulary, oral stories, riddles, and pronunciation examples, presented through engaging educational formats for youth and learners. Community elders and language contributors remain central to the process through a review and validation workflow, ensuring that cultural context, meaning, and pronunciation are preserved accurately before they become part of the official language resource.

Tech Stack

Figure 3.4 Tech Stack
The architecture uses a mobile-first approach where the application runs on Vite React and Ionic and is wrapped by Capacitor for iOS and Android delivery. On the client side, user actions such as authentication learning interactions translation and audio recording are first handled by local app logic and local storage to keep the experience responsive even with unstable connectivity. The app then synchronizes data to Supabase for identity relational data and file storage when connectivity is available. For AI inference, the current runtime path sends transcription requests to a local AI helper service which forwards audio processing to OmniASR while translation and coaching requests are routed through Supabase edge functions that coordinate model execution and return normalized results to the client.
Overall, we use this architecture because it works well and balances fast development with a structure that is ready for production. React, Ionic, and Capacitor allow our team to maintain a single codebase for both web and mobile platforms, while Supabase centralizes authentication, database management, storage, and serverless functions in one place that is relatively easy to scale and maintain. The architecture is also quite resilient. By handling some processes offline and synchronizing data later, the system remains usable even in low-connectivity situations, and queued synchronization helps prevent user data from being lost. At the same time, the design still leaves room to expand future AI features, such as deeper text-to-speech integration or story image generation, without requiring major changes to the core system.

Our team generated several feature ideas to address the problem. Prototype
The ideas were then developed into a mobile-first application prototype. The system integrates AI technologies such as speech recognition and translation to support interactive learning. The prototype allows users to explore stories, practice vocabulary, and contribute cultural content.
Test
User testing was conducted with 10 participants, including students and young professionals. Most participants understood the platform concept and enjoyed the interactive learning features. Their feedback suggested improvements such as clearer instructions and more vocabulary content for future development.

PROTOTYPE SHOWCASE
Key Features
Taleka provides several core features designed to support the preservation and learning of indigenous languages through storytelling, interactive learning, and community participation. These features aim to connect elders who hold cultural knowledge with younger generations who wish to learn and keep language alive.

Figure 5.1 Key Features
User Interface
The Taleka user interface is designed with a clean, culturally inspired layout to provide an intuitive and engaging user experience. The interface prioritizes accessibility and simplicity, allowing learners and elders to easily navigate the platform.

Color Palette
The Taleka color palette is designed to reflect cultural richness while maintaining a modern interface. The primary colors consist of red, yellow neon, and purple, which symbolize cultural vibrancy often found in traditional Southeast Asian art. These colors help create a strong visual identity and attract user attention.

Figure 5.2 Color Palette

Secondary colors such as blue teal and blue are used to provide balance and clarity within the interface. These colors represent calmness, trust, and stability, supporting readability and improving the overall user experience.

Font
Taleka uses the Satoshi typeface to create a clean, modern, and highly readable interface. The font is applied across different text hierarchies, including display titles, headings, and body text, ensuring visual consistency throughout the platform.

Figure 5.3 Font
Larger display styles are used for main titles and important sections, while heading styles organize content and improve readability. This typography system helps maintain clarity, accessibility, and a balanced visual structure within the user interface.

Feature Specification

Story Archive
Story Archive provides a digital storytelling experience where users can explore and read tales in the Semai language. Users can browse stories in the archive and view detailed information on the Reading Page, including the genre, author, and estimated reading duration before starting the story.

Elder Studio
Elder Studio is a storytelling recording space designed for elders to preserve and share their stories in Semai. Users can easily record oral histories, memories, and cultural knowledge using simple voice tools. The recordings are automatically stored in a digital archive, allowing younger generations to listen, learn, and reconnect with their linguistic and cultural heritage.

Interactive Story
Users can read illustrated stories with audio support in the Story section to enhance the storytelling experience. After the story ends, the Moral of the Story is displayed, highlighting the lesson and cultural values conveyed in the tale.

Language Garden
Language Garden is an interactive platform for learning in Semai. It provides activities such as vocabulary learning, Wordle puzzles, and quizzes to help users understand and practice new words. The system also includes progress levels such as Sprout, Sapling, and Tree to motivate learners and represent their language learning development.

Tavi AI Helper
AI Helper features Tavi, an AI language companion that supports interactive indigenous language learning. Users can communicate with Tavi through text on the Message and Chat pages or practice pronunciation using voice input on the Voice Page, helping them improve conversation skills and language understanding.

Translation
Translation Page enables users to translate text between indigenous languages, Malay, and English, helping learners understand vocabulary and improve accessibility in language learning.
